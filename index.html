Hi, I'm Carter! I research how people make moral judgments and statistical inferences in the Effective Altruism Psychology Lab at NYU and the Moral Psychology Research Lab at Harvard University. I enjoy thinking about a bunch of dorky topics, but recently I'm especially interested in rationality (particularly Bayesian reasoning, and the cognitive barriers that prevent people from making more consistent automatic statistical inferences) and existential risk (especially why people aren't more freaked out about the fact that humanity might go extinct this century , and what those of us who are appropriately freaked out can do).

Although I think a lot about stupidity and death, I also try to be a fun person! I love meeting new people and try to keep an autobiographical meet-me document updated here. I also longboard, occasionally write, and make really terrible computer music in my free time. Once in a while, I make a mistake, and you can give me anonymous feedback about how to make less of those here.

I made this website myself, by which I mean that I hardly made it at all and outsourced 80% of the coding work to an AI, which is how I do about 15% of my job these days, too. Soon, I'll update this page with more academic stuff (like my CV, research projects) and any writing or papers I publish.
